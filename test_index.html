Executive Summary
The Central Bank of Egypt has issued the first-ever 63-day Treasury notes worth EGP 150 billion on behalf of the Finance Ministry. The notes, which began accruing on July 16 and mature on September 17, yield the corridor's midpoint plus a 30-basis-point margin, or around 19.8% at the current corridor. The new tool gives the Treasury greater flexibility by linking the yield to the CBE's corridor rate, plus a fixed margin. This allows the government to share in any easing of interest rates instead of locking in today's high yields. The 63-day tenor aligns with current expectations that interest rates are headed lower in the coming period. The notes are likely to appeal to investors looking to hedge against a policy turn, particularly during periods when the direction of monetary policy is changing.
Future Implications
The introduction of floating-rate Treasury notes could have significant implications for Egypt's real estate development and housing markets. If interest rates do decrease, it could lead to increased demand for housing and commercial properties, potentially driving up property values. Additionally, the new tool could influence investment activity, with investors seeking to capitalize on the potential easing of interest rates. However, if the easing story stalls, the case for the tool may diminish, and the demand for these notes may decrease. As a result, developers and investors should closely monitor the development of this new funding tool and its impact on the market.

Executive Summary
