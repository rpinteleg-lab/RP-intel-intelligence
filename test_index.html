Executive Summary

Executive Summary
The Egyptian Businessmen Association's Committee for Real Estate Development and Contracting proposed a comprehensive system to regulate Egypt's real estate market. The system includes establishing an independent regulatory body to oversee licensing, supervision, and dispute resolution. The committee also suggested implementing a guarantee account system for off-plan sales, organizing and licensing real estate brokerage activities, and creating a specialized and rapid dispute resolution mechanism. The proposal is based on a comparative study of the experiences of the United Arab Emirates, particularly Dubai, and the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. The committee emphasized the importance of a unified regulatory body, guarantee accounts, and specialized dispute resolution mechanisms to protect the rights of all parties involved in the real estate market.
Future Implications
The proposed regulatory system could have significant implications for competing developers, land opportunities, and real estate investment in Egypt. The establishment of a guarantee account system could increase transparency and protect buyers' rights, while the regulation of brokerage activities could reduce fraud and enhance investor confidence. The creation of a specialized dispute resolution mechanism could also reduce the burden on the judicial system and provide a more efficient means of resolving disputes. Overall, the proposed system could contribute to a more stable and attractive real estate market in Egypt, which could have positive implications for the country's economic growth and development.
