Executive Summary
The Egyptian government is reducing its reliance on blanket sovereign guarantees for infrastructure projects, instead opting for project-specific guarantees and take-or-pay contracts. This shift is in line with OECD recommendations to shield the budget from hidden debt. The Finance Ministry has imposed a stricter cap on new guarantees, limiting contingent liabilities to EGP 560 billion in FY2026/27. This change has pushed the market towards more sustainable alternative contract models, with take-or-pay contracts providing a middle ground between budget protection and security for financiers. The new model has made the private sector and banks compete to finance projects, with companies like Redcon Construction already adapting to the new reality. The shift is expected to create a deeper sorting in the contracting and project finance market, with winning consortiums being those that can structure projects through take-or-pay models or smarter partial guarantees.
Future Implications
The reduction in sovereign guarantees will have significant implications for the Egyptian infrastructure market. It is expected to lead to a more sustainable and performance-based economy, with a greater emphasis on project-specific guarantees and take-or-pay contracts. This will create opportunities for developers and investors who can manage risk effectively and structure projects that can stand on their own economics. However, it also poses risks for those who rely heavily on state guarantees, as they will need to adapt to the new reality. The shift is also expected to lead to increased competition in the market, with private-sector players playing a growing role in infrastructure development.

Executive Summary
The Financial Regulatory Authority (FRA) has launched a new system to enforce final court and arbitration rulings on unlisted securities held at the central depository. The system requires the prevailing party to submit their final ruling or arbitration award to Misr for Central Clearing, Depository, and Registry (MCDR), along with necessary documentation. MCDR reviews the paperwork and gives the Egyptian Exchange (EGX) one week to flag any issues before moving the securities and settling the funds. This decision aims to resolve a long-running market bottleneck where mandatory orders stalled due to a lack of a clear execution pipeline. The system is part of the FRA's efforts to build a specialized dispute-resolution system for non-bank financial markets, utilizing existing economic courts and the Egyptian Center for Arbitration and Settlement of Non-Banking Financial Disputes (ECAS).
الملخص التنفيذي
أطلقت الهيئة العامة لتنظيم السوق المالية نظامًا جديدًا لإنفاذ أحكام المحاكم والتحكيم النهائية على الأوراق المالية غير المدرجة في البورصة المصرية. يتطلب النظام من الطرف المنتصر تقديم حكمه النهائي أو جائزة التحكيم إلى شركة مصر لالتسوية والايداع والقيد، إلى جانب المستندات اللازمة. وتقوم شركة مصر لالتسوية والايداع والقيد بمراجعة المستندات وتمنح البورصة المصرية أسبوعًا لتحديد أي مشاكل قبل نقل الأوراق المالية وتسوية الأموال. يهدف هذا النظام إلى حل عرقلة سوقية طويلة الأمد حيث تعثرت الأوامر الإلزامية بسبب عدم وجود خط أنابيب تنفيذ واضح. ويعد هذا النظام جزءًا من جهود الهيئة العامة لتنظيم السوق المالية لبناء نظام حل نزاعات متخصص للأسواق المالية غير المصرفية، باستخدام المحاكم الاقتصادية والمركز المصري للتحكيم وتسوية النزاعات المالية غير المصرفية.
