Egypt Limits Sovereign Guarantees How PPP financing rules are changing
Full Analysis
›
Executive Summary
The Egyptian government is phasing out blanket sovereign guarantees for infrastructure projects, instead opting for project-specific guarantees and take-or-pay contracts. This shift, recommended by the OECD, aims to reduce contingent liabilities and protect the state budget. The new approach has pushed the market towards more sustainable alternative contract models, with banks and private-sector players competing to finance projects. The government plans to lower the ceiling on government guarantees to EGP 560 billion in FY2026/27, down from EGP 740 billion in the current fiscal year. This change is expected to create a deeper sorting in the contracting and project finance market, with winning consortiums being those that can structure projects through take-or-pay models or smarter partial guarantees. The shift also fits into a broader plan to rein in borrowing and reduce the state's crowding out of the private sector in the domestic debt market. Developers, such as Redcon Construction, are already testing the new reality in current infrastructure tenders, with a focus on seawater desalination plant projects. The new guarantee model has made the private sector and banks compete to finance projects, with the state redirecting its investment focus toward power and renewables, nearly doubling electricity and renewables investments to EGP 136.3 billion in FY2025/26.
Future Implications
The shift away from blanket sovereign guarantees is expected to have significant implications for Egypt and the MENA region. The new approach is likely to increase competition among contractors and financiers, driving innovation and better value for money. However, it also poses risks, such as increased project costs and reduced access to financing for smaller contractors. The focus on take-or-pay contracts and project-specific guarantees is expected to create new opportunities for private-sector investment in infrastructure projects, particularly in the power and renewables sector. Additionally, the reduction in direct contract awards and reliance on exceptions in the public procurement law is expected to strengthen market competition and increase transparency.

FRA Launches System for Enforcing Court Rulings الهيئة العامة للاستثمار تنشئ نظاما جديدا لتنفيذ الأحكام القضائية
Summary
›
Executive Summary
The Financial Regulatory Authority (FRA) has launched a new system to enforce final court and arbitration rulings on unlisted securities held at the central depository. The system involves the prevailing party submitting their final ruling or arbitration award to Misr for Central Clearing, Depository, and Registry (MCDR), along with necessary documentation. MCDR then reviews the paperwork and gives the Egyptian Exchange (EGX) one week to flag any issues before moving the securities and settling the funds. This system aims to address a long-running market bottleneck where mandatory orders for the sale or transfer of unlisted securities stalled due to a lack of a clear execution pipeline. The FRA's decision is part of its efforts to build a specialized dispute-resolution system for non-bank financial markets. Last year, the FRA's former head, Mohamed Farid, argued that existing economic courts and the Egyptian Center for Arbitration and Settlement of Non-Banking Financial Disputes (ECAS) could be utilized instead of establishing new capital-market courts. The current FRA head, Islam Azzam, is now ensuring that judicial rulings result in shares changing hands.
الملخص التنفيذي
أطلقت الهيئة العامة للاستثمار نظاما جديدا لتنفيذ الأحكام القضائية النهائية والتحكيم على الأوراق المالية غير المدرجة في البورصة المصرية. يتضمن النظام تقديم الطرف المنتصر لقرار التحكيم النهائي أو جائزة التحكيم إلى شركة مصر للتسوية والايداع والقيد، إلى جانب المستندات اللازمة. ثم تقوم شركة مصر للتسوية والايداع والقيد بمراجعة المستندات وتمنح البورصة المصرية أسبوعا لتحديد أي مشاكل قبل نقل الأوراق المالية وتسوية الأموال. يهدف النظام إلى حل عرقلة سوقية طويلة الأمد حيث تتعطل الأوامر الإلزامية لبيع أو نقل الأوراق المالية غير المدرجة بسبب عدم وجود خط سير تنفيذي واضح. يأتي قرار الهيئة في إطار جهودها لبناء نظام متخصص لحل النزاعات المالية غير المصرفية. في العام الماضي، أشار رئيس الهيئة السابق، محمد فريد، إلى أن المحاكم الاقتصادية والمركز المصري للتحكيم وتسوية النزاعات المالية غير المصرفية يمكن أن تستخدم بدلا من إنشاء محاكم سوق رأس المال جديدة. والآن، يضمن رئيس الهيئة الحالي، إسلام عزام، أن تؤدي الأحكام القضائية إلى نقل ملكية الأسهم.
