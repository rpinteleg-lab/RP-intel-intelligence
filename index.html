Executive Summary
The Egyptian government has finalized the second iteration of its State Ownership Policy, updating the 2022 framework to adopt a company-by-company portfolio approach. Prime Minister Mostafa Madbouly announced that a detailed executive program will be finalized by September 30. The policy groups divestment targets into three tiers, with airports, banks, and telecoms as urgent priorities. The first SOP generated USD 5.9 billion in direct proceeds across 19 transactions. State ownership will become selective, limited to cases with sovereign, strategic, or social justification, with the Sovereign Fund of Egypt managing eligible assets.

Executive Summary
The US Senate Foreign Relations Committee has approved a bipartisan bill that identifies Egypt's energy infrastructure, including the planned Greece-Egypt electricity interconnection and LNG facilities, as key links in the India-Middle East-Europe Economic Corridor. The Eastern Mediterranean Gateway Act positions Egypt, Greece, Cyprus, and Israel as a strategic gateway and seeks to institutionalize US support for greater connectivity across the region. The bill frames the corridor as a strategic alternative to Chinese-backed infrastructure initiatives. The proposed bill requires approval from the full Senate and House before becoming law. The initiative aims to enhance regional connectivity, with Egypt's LNG facilities and the Gregy interconnection project playing a crucial role.
